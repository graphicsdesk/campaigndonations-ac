Micah Lasher, Jordan Wright receive most donations at or above $1,000 in state assembly District 69 and 70 races

Lasher received 163 donations at or above $1,000 and Wright received 72

By Isha Banerjee

Graphic by Alison Chiu

November 4, 2024

Anna Fedorova / Senior Staff Photographer

Micah Lasher and Jordan Wright, respective winners of the New York State Assembly District 69 and District 70 Democratic primary races, received the most campaign donations at or above $1,000 among their competitors in their respective campaigns, according to a Spectator analysis.

The candidates with the second highest number of votes in both districts, Eli Northrup in District 69 and Maria Ordoñez in District 70, received significantly fewer donations.

After winning the primaries, Wright is heavily favored in his race against Republican candidate Seson Adams, while Lasher will not face Republican opposition in the general election.

Election day is on Nov. 5, and 19 poll sites in Community Board 9 will be open from 6 a.m. to 9 p.m.

Lasher received a total of 163 donations that were $1,000 or above, compared to Northrup’s 42. Most of Lasher’s donations were from individual donors, and 11 came from political committees and PACs. In total, Lasher received $470,389.50 in donations.

Wright received 72 donations of $1,000 or more, compared to Ordoñez’s seven. Eighteen of Wright’s donations came from political committees and PACs. The District 70 primary winner received $248,217.30 in total.
